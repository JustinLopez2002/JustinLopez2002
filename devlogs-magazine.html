DevLogs Magazine

My colleg had an InDesign unit. For its exam, we were required to make a magazine with a cover, a back cover, a table of contents, and two articles. I based this magazine around game development, featuring testimonies from fictional game developers about fictional games. For the cover, I experimented with blending, brightness, and glitch effects. I figured I could differentiate this magazine from real gaming magazines by focusing on game development.

DevLogs Journal

One of the exam's requirements was to attach some kind of bonus feature. I tried my hand at interactive forms in PDFs by making a journal, meant for magazine readers interested in developing their own game. Its features include note-taking pages, diagram-drawing pages, and to-do lists users can fill in themselves. Initially, it was made in InDesign. However, when making this website, I went back and remade the interactive forms in Acrobat.
